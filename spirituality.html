Spirituality

The Spirituality of Employer Relations

Loyola University embodies the Christian principles of social justice that have under-girded the vocational decisions of my life, first as a cross-cultural worker, as a converted Catholic, and later as a higher education professional. Loyola is forefront in producing servant-leaders through its rigorous education and social commitment of service for the greatest Glory of God. Arrupe College is the flagship that advances the Jesuit missionary vocation of service to those who have been forgotten by the education system. The Strategic Plan 2020 expresses and reflects my deepest convictions of what a transformative education is like.

I am a committed secular Franciscan who has been deeply influenced by Ignatian spirituality. I am a convinced believer that it is in the University where our students are able to follow the common human vocation of seeking wisdom, living beauty, and practicing justice, finding the imprint of God in all things created. I do not consider the University my career but my vocation, through which we are called to be instruments of peace, justice, and truth, reconciling our broken society with God. Such a reconciliation invariably requires us to take the University to our under-served communities as an imperative act of justice. In this regard it is important to clearly state that we do not serve the marginalized because we regard ourselves as better in some way, or because somehow without us they would not be able to get ahead by themselves. Nothing can be further from reality than such misconception of the Jesuit higher education vocation, which, in it’s perfect expression, is a walking alongside. As Fr. Gutierrez, O.P. so aptly describes, we serve alongside the underserved because they are living under circumstances against God’s will, and that is an imperative to action for those who have received the vocation to walk towards the kingdom of God in accordance with the Jesuit charisma. We cannot get used to what God regards as intolerable; the suffering of the innocent.

With over 15 years of experience working with emerging adults both internationally and here in Chicago, I offer compassionate, professional guidance that inspires students to find their true vocations, not just occupations. I specialize in recruiting, mentoring, and advocating for first-generation students from diverse ethnic backgrounds and immigration statuses to succeed academically, transfer successfully, and to land profitable internships and jobs. I know how to advocate within both college and professional structures to establish solid institutional and personal partnerships, thus fulfilling Arrupe’s promise to each student to secure them meaningful internships. I am committed to Loyola’s larger Mission and Vision, and I look forward to reaching the Strategic Plan 2020’s goals.I do not hesitate to pledge my faithful service to such a noble mission.
